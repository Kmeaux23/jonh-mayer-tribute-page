John Clayton Mayer is an American singer, songwriter, and guitarist born on October 16, 1977, in Fairfield County, Connecticut. John is regarded as one of his generation’s most accomplished musicians. His soulful voice and talent have earned him a large fan base and praise all over the world. However, as we all know, there is a lot more to this talented star, so let’s look at some interesting facts you probably didn’t know about him.


Interesting Facts:

  • John Mayer was born to Richard Mayer, who was a school principal, and to his mother, Margaret, who was a middle-school English teacher.
  • At the age of 13, his father got him a guitar for rent. To practice the instrument, Mayer started taking elementary lessons from the owner of a guitar shop in his neighborhood.
  • His love of Blues music bloomed when he got a Stevie Ray Vaughan cassette from one of his neighbors.
  • He suffered from cardiac dysrhythmia at the age of 17, for which he had to be admitted to a hospital for a week.
  • According to Mayer, it was then he realized his love and interest in music and then was born the songwriter in him.
  • John's hobbies include Stand up comedy, singing, playing instruments, and writing songs.
  • He has a hobby of collecting watches.
  • “The Filthy Rich Guide” estimates Mayer's net worth to be around $40 million, which makes his watch collection worth at least 25 percent of his wealth.
  • His major works include his debut album 'Room for Squares,' which garnered positive feedback from renowned critics.
  • It is a significant achievement that his second album, 'Heavier Things,' topped the 'US Billboard 200' charts, and hundreds of thousands of copies were sold.
  • Then his third studio album 'Continuum' sold millions of copies worldwide and was ranked 2nd on the Billboard charts. It even earned him a Grammy for the 'Best Pop Vocal Album.' Mayer also received two Grammy Awards for 'Daughters' under the categories 'Song of the Year' and 'Best Male Pop Vocal Performance' in 2005. 'Battle Studies' has also been one of his best works.
